Welcome to my homepage! Here I'll try to share things that interest me.

  • I'm currently finishing my PhD studies at the University of Alicante. My research is mostly focused on trying to understand the inner workings of neural networks, mechanistic interpretability and its applications.
  • Before getting into deep dearning/ML I got a physics degree. During that time I discovered ML and loved the combination of programming/math/problem solving abilities.

Things/projects that I find interesting:

  • Tinygrad is a cool deep learning framework. The main philosophy behind this project is to keep it simple so that it is easy to add new accelerators. It also implements other concepts such as lazyness which makes it really interesting.
  • The Vesuvius Challenge is an ML competition to read ancient carbonized scrolls found at Herculaneum. A part of one scroll has already been recovered, so if you want to be the first in 2000 years to read some ancient text, consider contributing to the project!
  • The Recovering Overthinker publishes philosophical and psychological content mostly applied to living. Their posts really made me think and I have discovered lots of interesting topics to read and learn about.
